## Purpose
Ready-to-use, tested plant combinations for specific garden conditions.
These are the "planting matrices" from Jerry's strategic analysis — repeatable,
modular designs that non-experts can implement with confidence.
Each matrix is validated for a specific soil/aspect combination.

## Prompt injection note
Injected when user has specific condition combination AND requests design ideas.
These are offered as starting points, not prescriptions — the AI adapts them
to the user's specific goals, safety requirements, and style preferences.

---

## How to use a matrix

Each matrix is a layered plant community for a specific area (typically 4–6m²).
It can be scaled: multiply quantities for larger beds.
Plants are listed by layer: Tall (>1m), Mid (40cm–1m), Low (<40cm), Seasonal.

**Spacing guidance:**
- Tall layer: 60–90cm centres
- Mid layer: 45–60cm centres
- Low/ground layer: 30–45cm centres
- Seasonal bulbs: 15–20cm centres, planted through the permanent layer

## Matrix 1: Sunny, free-draining (south/west aspect, sandy or loam soil)
**For:** Mediterranean-style or naturalistic sunny border
**Season:** April–November

### Plant list (for 6m² bed):
**Tall (back/centre):**
- Salvia nemorosa 'Caradonna' × 3 (violet-purple spikes; June–August; 60cm)
- Verbena bonariensis × 3 (transparent purple; July–frost; 1.5m)
- Agastache 'Black Adder' × 2 (anise-scented; blue; July–Sept; 90cm)

**Mid:**
- Echinacea purpurea 'Magnus' × 3 (pink; July–Sept + winter heads; 90cm)
- Penstemon 'Husker Red' × 3 (white-pink; June–Sept; 60cm)
- Allium sphaerocephalon × 9 bulbs (claret; July; 60cm)

**Low:**
- Nepeta 'Six Hills Giant' × 3 (lavender; May–Oct; 60cm)
- Sedum 'Matrona' × 2 (pink; Aug–Oct + winter heads; 50cm)
- Geranium 'Rozanne' × 3 (blue; June–Oct; 30cm)

**Seasonal:**
- Allium 'Purple Sensation' × 15 bulbs (April–May)
- Tulipa 'Queen of Night' × 20 bulbs (deep purple, April)

## Matrix 2: Sunny clay (south/west aspect, heavy clay soil)
**Key consideration:** Clay stays wet in winter — avoid plants that rot
in waterlogged conditions. Clay's nutrient richness supports lush growth.
**Season:** April–November

### Plant list (for 6m²):
**Tall:**
- Helenium 'Moerheim Beauty' × 3 (copper-red; July–Sept; 90cm — loves clay)
- Persicaria amplexicaulis 'Firetail' × 2 (red; June–Oct; 1.2m — thrives in clay)
- Rosa 'Gertrude Jekyll' × 1 (deep pink, scented; June–Oct; 1.2m)

**Mid:**
- Astrantia major 'Ruby Wedding' × 3 (dark red; June–Aug; 60cm)
- Geranium 'Patricia' × 3 (magenta; June–Sept; 60cm)
- Rudbeckia fulgida 'Goldsturm' × 3 (yellow; Aug–Oct; 60cm)

**Low:**
- Alchemilla mollis × 5 (acid-green froth; May–July; self-seeds — edit)
- Ajuga reptans 'Atropurpurea' × 5 (purple foliage; April; 15cm)
- Pulmonaria 'Blue Ensign' × 3 (blue; March–April; 30cm — early!)

**Seasonal:**
- Camassia leichtlinii × 20 bulbs (blue-white spires; May; 90cm — loves clay)
- Narcissus 'Ice Follies' × 15 (cream; March–April)

**For:** The classic UK "problem" garden — actually rich with opportunity

## Matrix 4: Dry shade (under trees, north-facing wall)
**The hardest combination — but these plants are genuinely reliable**
**Season:** March–September (limited)

### Plant list (for 4m²):
**Tall (relatively):**
- Dryopteris filix-mas × 3 (male fern; architectural; semi-evergreen; 80cm)
- Polystichum setiferum × 2 (soft shield fern; evergreen; elegant; 60cm)

**Mid:**
- Epimedium × warleyense × 5 (orange flowers; excellent dry shade; 30cm)
- Geranium macrorrhizum 'Bevan's Variety' × 4 (scented foliage; pink; May; 30cm)
- Liriope muscari × 5 (evergreen; purple spikes; Sept–Oct; 30cm)

**Low/ground cover:**
- Pachysandra terminalis × 12 (evergreen; white; April; 25cm)
- Vinca minor 'La Grave' × 6 (dark blue; March–May; evergreen — space needed)

**Important notes:**
- Avoid bark mulch here — it dries out further. Leaf mould mulch preferred.
- Water thoroughly during establishment (first 2 seasons) — then largely self-sufficient.
- Accept limitations — flowering will be limited; foliage and texture is the display.

## Matrix 5: Wildlife garden (any aspect, any soil — adaptable)
**For:** Maximum ecological value; bees, butterflies, birds
**Season:** April–November

### Plant list (adapt by aspect — sunny version):
**Tall:**
- Verbena bonariensis × 4 (transparent; butterflies; July–frost; 1.5m)
- Inula magnifica × 2 (large yellow daisies; bees; July–Aug; 1.8m)
- Sanguisorba officinalis × 3 (dark bobbles; bees; July–Sept; 1m)

**Mid:**
- Echinacea purpurea 'Magnus' × 4 (bees + goldfinches; July–Sept; 90cm)
- Scabiosa 'Butterfly Blue' × 3 (lavender; butterflies; June–Oct; 40cm)
- Achillea 'Terracotta' × 3 (flat heads; hoverflies; June–Aug; 80cm)
- Knautia macedonica × 4 (claret; bees; June–Aug; 60cm)

**Low:**
- Nepeta × faassenii × 4 (bees; May–Oct; 40cm)
- Thymus serpyllum × 6 (bees; June–July; 5cm — paths/edges)
- Origanum vulgare 'Herrenhausen' × 3 (bees; July–Sept; 40cm)

**Seasonal:**
- Allium hollandicum 'Purple Sensation' × 15 (bees; May; 80cm)
- Phacelia tanacetifolia × direct sow annually (bees; June–Sept; 40cm)
- Calendula officinalis × direct sow (pollinators; June–frost)

## Matrix 6: Year-round interest (south/west, loam or clay)
**For:** Gardeners who want something happening in every season
**Season:** January–December (designed for it)

### Spring layer (Jan–May):
- Eranthis hyemalis (winter aconite; Jan–Feb)
- Helleborus × hybridus 'Anna's Red' × 3 (Jan–April; 45cm)
- Narcissus 'Tête-à-tête' × 20 (March; 15cm)
- Tulipa 'Ballerina' × 20 (April; 60cm)
- Allium 'Purple Sensation' × 15 (May; 80cm)

### Summer layer:
- Rosa 'The Generous Gardener' × 1 (June–Oct; 1.5m — repeat flowering)
- Salvia nemorosa 'Caradonna' × 4 (June–Aug; 60cm)
- Nepeta 'Six Hills Giant' × 3 (May–Oct; 60cm)
- Geranium 'Rozanne' × 3 (June–Oct; 30cm)

### Autumn/winter structure:
- Cotinus coggygria 'Grace' × 1 (smoke bush; fiery autumn; 2m — allow space)
- Echinacea × 3 (seed heads through winter; goldfinches)
- Calamagrostis 'Karl Foerster' × 3 (buff plumes; stand through winter)
- Sedum 'Matrona' × 3 (pink heads; age to buff; Jan–March if not cut)
